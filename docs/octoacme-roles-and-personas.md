# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers advocate for the end user throughout the product lifecycle. They translate user needs into designs, prototypes, and usability feedback that guide development decisions.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and translate findings into design requirements
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and design specifications
- Participate in backlog refinement to ensure usability is considered early
- Provide usability feedback during reviews and acceptance testing
- Collaborate with developers on accessible, consistent UI implementation

### Goals
- Ensure features meet user needs and usability standards
- Reduce rework by surfacing design issues before development begins
- Improve product quality through continuous user feedback

### Interaction Points
- Works with **Product Managers** to align designs with product vision and priorities
- Collaborates with **Developers** to validate implementation against design specs
- Supports **Project Managers** in ensuring usability acceptance criteria are met
- Engages **Key Stakeholder Groups** during design reviews and usability validation

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and prototype walkthroughs at sprint boundaries
- Async feedback via design tools (e.g., Figma comments) and PR reviews
- Participation in backlog refinement and sprint planning sessions

---

## Scrum Master / Agile Facilitator

### Role Summary
The Scrum Master coaches the team on agile practices, facilitates ceremonies, removes impediments, and maintains the team's delivery rhythm to ensure consistent, predictable output.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate daily standups, sprint planning, reviews, and retrospectives
- Identify and remove blockers that slow team progress
- Coach team members on agile principles and practices
- Track and communicate team velocity and sprint health
- Shield the team from unplanned interruptions and scope creep

### Goals
- Maintain a sustainable, predictable delivery cadence
- Foster continuous improvement through regular retrospectives
- Enable the team to self-organize and solve problems effectively

### Interaction Points
- Supports **Project Managers** in coordinating team activities and flagging risks
- Aligns with **Product Managers** to ensure the backlog is ready for each sprint
- Coaches **Developers** on agile practices and sustainable work habits
- Coordinates with **Key Stakeholder Groups** to manage expectations during sprint reviews

### Typical Communication
- Facilitates all agile ceremonies (standups, planning, retrospectives, reviews)
- Escalates persistent blockers to Project Manager or Product Lead
- Maintains sprint boards and communicates progress metrics to the team

---

## Release Manager

### Role Summary
Release Managers coordinate release planning and deployment activities, ensuring features reach production safely, predictably, and with clear communication to all stakeholders.

### Responsibilities
- Define and maintain the release calendar and deployment windows
- Coordinate readiness reviews across development, QA, and operations
- Ensure release notes, runbooks, and rollback plans are prepared and reviewed
- Communicate release schedules and status to stakeholders and support teams
- Oversee post-release verification and coordinate incident response if needed

### Goals
- Deliver releases with minimal disruption to production environments
- Ensure all stakeholders are informed before, during, and after each release
- Reduce release risk through consistent process adherence and documentation

### Interaction Points
- Collaborates with **Project Managers** to align release windows with project milestones
- Works with **Developers** and QA to verify release readiness and acceptance criteria
- Coordinates with **Key Stakeholder Groups** for approvals and release communications
- Engages **Scrum Master / Agile Facilitator** to plan releases within sprint cadence

### Typical Communication
- Release readiness meetings prior to each deployment window
- Release notes and announcements distributed to stakeholders and support teams
- Post-release retrospective notes and incident reports when applicable

---

## Key Stakeholder Groups

### Role Summary
Key Stakeholder Groups represent the business, customer, and operational interests in the project. They provide requirements, review progress, approve deliverables, and escalate business risks as needed.

### Responsibilities
- Provide clear business requirements and success criteria at project initiation
- Review and approve key deliverables at defined milestones
- Escalate business risks and decisions that require leadership input
- Participate in sprint reviews and provide timely feedback on delivered features
- Support change management and communication within their business areas

### Goals
- Ensure project outcomes align with business objectives and customer needs
- Maintain visibility into project progress and risks
- Enable fast, informed decision-making to keep delivery on track

### Interaction Points
- Engaged by **Product Managers** for requirements gathering and backlog prioritization
- Consulted by **Project Managers** for milestone approvals and risk escalations
- Invited by **Scrum Master / Agile Facilitator** to sprint reviews and demos
- Informed by **Release Managers** of upcoming deployments and release content

### Typical Communication
- Milestone review meetings and sprint demos
- Weekly or bi-weekly status updates from the Project Manager
- Approval workflows for major deliverables and release sign-off
