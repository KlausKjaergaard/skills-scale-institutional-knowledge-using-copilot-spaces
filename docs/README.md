# OctoAcme Project Management Docs

Welcome to the central hub for OctoAcme's project management process documentation. This README provides a brief overview of how OctoAcme manages projects and links to all major process documents. It is intended to serve as the starting point for both new and existing team members seeking to understand our workflows, roles, and quality standards.

---

## Project Management Process Summary

OctoAcme employs a comprehensive, structured approach to project management, aiming to deliver customer value through iterative and transparent workflows. Central to the methodology is the use of project boards and well-defined process documents, covering all stages from project initiation to retrospective. Projects begin with a clear one-pager that defines the problem, objectives, stakeholders, and success criteria. Once an initiative is approved, planning activities break down work into shippable increments, prioritize the backlog, and establish release milestones, ensuring requirements and risks are systematically captured and managed throughout.

Key roles within OctoAcme include the Project Manager (PM), Product Manager (PdM), Developers, QA/Testers, and Stakeholders. The PM facilitates coordination, risk management, and communication, keeping delivery on track and documentation up to date. The PdM owns the product vision and prioritizes features based on data-driven decisions. Developers execute work against defined acceptance criteria, participate in code reviews, and maintain documentation and tests, while QA validates quality through test plans and acceptance checks. Stakeholders provide input and approvals at critical junctures, fostering shared ownership.

Communication is structured and frequent: weekly syncs between PM and PdM, twice-weekly delivery team standups, and monthly stakeholder updates. Standups and demos ensure alignment and rapid escalation of blockers, with formal escalation paths defined for high-impact risks and issues (team → PM → Product Lead → Sponsor). Status updates use templates for clarity, and a single source of truth is maintained for project progress.

Quality assurance is embedded at every stage. The process mandates unit, integration, and end-to-end smoke tests, with security scanning integrated into CI pipelines. Releases follow a standardized checklist covering PR approval, CI passes, release notes, and rollback plans, ensuring safe deployments and rapid incident response. Continuous improvement is driven through retrospectives after each sprint or release, capturing learnings and action items that feed back into process documentation and backlog refinement.
